Course Description

We create 2.5 quintillion bytes of data everyday. We have generated the 90% of world's data only in the last two years [source: IBM]. Traditional data processing applications are inadequate in terms of storing, searching, transferring and analyzing datasets that are so large and/or complex. "Big Data" refers to such large and complex datasets that needs to be stored, queried and analyzed.

Companies and organizations have already started to embrace the "Big Data", simply because its potential to understand the reality and predict the future better. However, one must consider the different aspects of dealing with Big Data including storage, search and analytics.

In this course, we will learn the importance of embracing "Big Data". We will:

  • Understand Big Data from a business perspective.
  • Understand Big Data from a technical perspective such as how to store, process and analyze a large amount of data?
  • Understand Data Science and its practices.
  • Understand methods and tools used in industry to deal with Big Data.

As a part of the course, you will have weekly assignments, a midterm, a final and a group project. More details about the project will be given in class.

Although you will learn a lot about Big Data, it is important to note that this course is meant as an introduction to get you start understanding of Big Data phenemonen. Hence do not expect to be a data scientist or Hadoop expert by only taking this course. However, this course will jump start your effort of becoming an expert in various aspects of Big Data.
